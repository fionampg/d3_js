In Perspective: Poverty vs. Healthcare

Correlation Discovered Between Lack of Healthcare and Poverty Level in U.S. States

There is a correlation between the lack of healthcare and the poverty levels. As shown in the chart above, the higher the percentage of lack of healthcare in U.S. states, the higher the poverty rate.

For example, Mississippi has a the highest percentage of poverty levels, as well as the highest percentage of lack of healthcare. On the other hand, Massachusettes has low poverty levels as well as low lack of healthcare percentages (or in other terms, more access to healthcare) .

There are some outliers, such as Washington, DC. In Washington, DC, the there is more access to healthcare, but there are a lot of people in poverty.
